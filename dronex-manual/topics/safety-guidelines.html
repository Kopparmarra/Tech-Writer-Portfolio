Fly in Open Areas

Follow these instructions to ensure safe and responsible flying by staying in open areas, avoiding no-fly zones, and adhering to local regulations.

  1. Never fly in No-Fly zones.

    No-fly zones are areas where drone operations are restricted or prohibited, such as near airports, military bases, or densely populated areas. Check local regulations and use drone apps to identify restricted zones before flying.

  2. Always fly in open areas.

    Flying in open areas reduces the risk of accidents and ensures the safety of people, property, and wildlife. Open areas provide better visibility and more room to maneuver your drone.

  3. Maintain a safe distance from people and property.

    Ensure your drone is at least 30 meters (100 feet) away from people, vehicles, and buildings to prevent accidents or damage. Avoid flying over crowds or private property without permission.

  4. Check weather conditions before flying.

    Adverse weather conditions, such as strong winds, rain, or fog, can affect the performance and stability of your drone. Always check the weather forecast and avoid flying in unfavorable conditions.

  5. Adhere to local drone regulations.

    Familiarize yourself with the drone laws and regulations in your area. Obtain any necessary permits or licenses and follow altitude and distance restrictions to ensure compliance.
