Understanding Measurement Values

Explanation of systolic, diastolic, and pulse readings.

Learn to interpret your measurement values.

Interpreting Systolic and Diastolic Readings

Systolic and diastolic readings are critical indicators of your blood pressure. The systolic value represents the pressure in your arteries when your heart beats, while the diastolic value represents the pressure when your heart is at rest between beats.

Normal blood pressure is typically around 120/80 mmHg. Values above 140/90 mmHg may indicate hypertension, while values below 90/60 mmHg may indicate hypotension. Always consult a healthcare professional for accurate diagnosis and advice.

Understanding Pulse Readings

Your pulse rate, measured in beats per minute (BPM), reflects your heart rate. A normal resting pulse rate for adults ranges from 60 to 100 BPM. Values outside this range may require medical attention.

Warning: Extreme Values

Warning:

Important: If your readings fall into the extreme ranges, such as a systolic value above 180 mmHg or below 80 mmHg, or a diastolic value above 120 mmHg or below 50 mmHg, this could indicate a critical health issue. Similarly, a pulse rate above 120 BPM or below 40 BPM is considered extreme.

Action Required: If you encounter such extreme values, seek immediate medical attention. These readings may indicate a life-threatening condition that requires urgent care.
